# Anti-AI Checklist

Use this after drafting a scene or chapter opening.

## Core Principle

Aim for this combination:

- plotting and payoff can be ambitious
- sentence-level language should stay direct, readable, and commercially natural
- the prose should sound like a practiced web novelist, not like an essay generator trying to sound impressive

## Fast Pass

Check for these and revise if present:

- opening paragraph summarizes context before conflict starts
- three or more consecutive paragraphs have similar length and cadence
- dialogue sounds more articulate than stressed people usually sound
- emotional state is named before behavior, word choice, or avoidance reveals it
- paragraph endings often sound like mini conclusions
- metaphors feel transferable to any story
- exposition arrives earlier than consequence
- the scene could be shortened a lot just by removing adjectives and mood lines
- dialogue exists only to decorate tone rather than push conflict or information
- every line sounds clean, complete, and socially risk-free

## Useful Constraints To Add

These usually help:

- keep sentences mostly short or medium unless a specific voice needs longer flow
- let dialogue stand in its own paragraph in most cases
- prefer white话、口语化书面语、职业细节、阶层细节 over polished narration
- use humor, banter, or reversal only when the genre and characters can carry it
- respect hard constraints, names, facts, and established setting; do not improvise new lore
- output only正文, not explanation, summary, or chapter-title repetition

## Use With Caution

These are sometimes helpful, but only conditionally:

- profanity: use only when the character, relationship, and platform tolerance support it
- internet slang or hot memes: use only when they sound native to the speaker and will not age badly in two chapters
- exaggerated reversals: good for hook density, bad when they cheapen character logic
- anti-cyberpunk language bans: useful for mainstream现实、言情、都市、古言 lanes, but not for actual科幻 or赛博题材

## Cut Or Replace

Common AI-flavored habits to remove:

- `他心中一震`
- `空气仿佛凝固了`
- `眼神复杂`
- `嘴角勾起一抹弧度`
- `一种难以言喻的感觉`
- generic summary lines that explain what the reader already saw

Also treat these as warning lights when they appear too often:

- too many `仿佛 / 如同 / 像是 / 顿时 / 立刻 / 连忙`
- repeated `嘴角勾起 / 微微挑眉 / 深吸一口气 / 眼中闪过`
- stock "impact" verbs such as `炸开` used without concrete scene value
- faux-serious atmospheric words that do not change action cost

Do not create a gigantic hard blacklist. Use frequency and context as the real test.

Do not ban these absolutely, but treat them as warning lights.

## Revision Moves

Use one or more of these:

- replace an abstract feeling with a physical or social reaction
- interrupt a polished line of dialogue with hesitation or deflection
- swap one generic detail for a profession-specific, class-specific, or local detail
- cut the last sentence of a paragraph if it merely explains the paragraph
- merge or split sentences to create rhythm contrast
- let one character misread another instead of making emotion perfectly legible
- convert one decorative environment sentence into one action-changing detail
- if a joke exists, make sure it comes from attitude, timing, or relationship pressure instead of random meme-dropping

## Humanizing Constraint Prompt

Use this when revising:

```text
Revise the scene to feel less machine-written and more like an experienced commercial novelist.
Keep the plot and information, but reduce abstract emotional labeling, neat paragraph symmetry, and explanatory wrap-up sentences.
Make at least 20% of the improvement come from deletion rather than addition.
Preserve readability and platform fit.
Keep the language plainspoken and natural rather than literary.
Do not force vulgarity, memes, or faux-gritty diction.
Keep dialogue useful and scene-driving.
Output only the revised scene.
```

## Final Test

The scene is stronger if:

- the reader can infer mood from behavior without being told directly
- at least one detail feels socially observed rather than statistically likely
- the scene ends on tension, curiosity, or discomfort instead of summary
- two characters could be identified from dialogue alone in at least one exchange
