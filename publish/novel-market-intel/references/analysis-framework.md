# Analysis Framework

Use this framework after gathering chart data.

## 1. Normalize Every Book

For each visible title, infer only what the page supports:

- genre
- subgenre
- reader segment
- title syntax pattern
- blurb promise
- protagonist identity
- core conflict
- novelty lever
- emotional reward

## 2. Group By Hook Family

Typical hook families:

- power climb
- system/golden finger
- rebirth or time reset
- marriage/relationship contract
- revenge comeback
- workplace or family conflict
- fandom/crossover wish fulfillment
- apocalypse survival
- farming/business-building
- mystery or rule-based suspense

Count repeated families across platforms and time windows.

## 3. Read Packaging, Not Just Plot

Focus on how works are sold:

- does the title announce the cheat, status, or reversal
- does the blurb foreground conflict in one sentence
- is the emotional promise clear within 30 words
- does the story feel high-concept or immersion-first

## 4. Detect Pacing Norms

Infer expected pacing from charts and platform ecology:

- free-reading platforms often reward immediate conflict and fast confirmation loops
- paid long-serial platforms tolerate more setup if the world promise is strong
- female-channel ecosystems often reward relationship voltage and emotional scene density

## 5. Separate Heat From Opportunity

Not every hot trope is worth following.

Use three labels:

- `Red ocean`: already saturated, hard to stand out without a major twist
- `Warm lane`: established demand with room for stronger execution
- `White space`: adjacent need with visible demand but limited polished supply

## 6. Convert To Writing Directions

Each direction should include:

- target platform
- target audience
- title style
- opening scene model
- protagonist archetype
- first-20-chapter promise
- one freshness twist
- one anti-staleness rule

## 7. Recommended Output Sentences

Use sentences like:

- `Observed on 2026-04-18, 番茄's visible fantasy recommendations leaned toward immediate identity reversal and fast reward loops.`
- `Inferred from repeated romance packaging on 晋江, readers are rewarding emotionally legible pair dynamics more than ornate worldbuilding.`
- `Hypothesis: a workplace-plus-family-conflict hybrid may outperform pure sweet-romance if executed with sharper scene goals.`

## 8. What Not To Do

- Do not claim absolute market share without a source.
- Do not confuse platform promotion with genuine durable demand.
- Do not reduce a hot book to only its plot gimmick; packaging and scene rhythm matter as much as premise.
